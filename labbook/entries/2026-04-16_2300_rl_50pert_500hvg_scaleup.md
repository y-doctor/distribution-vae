# GRPO perturbation-classifier scaled to 500 HVGs x 50 perts
**Date**: 2026-04-16 23:00 UTC
**Duration**: ~2 hours
**Goal**: Scale up the GRPO classifier from 100g/10p to 500g/50p and assess performance with a proper held-out evaluation + confusion matrix.

## What I did

1. Regenerated the mini dataset with `scripts/make_mini_dataset.py --n-genes 500 --n-perts 50 --keep-controls`. New file: `data/mini_perturb_seq_500g_50p_ntc.h5ad` — 37,189 cells × 500 genes × 51 perts (50 knockdowns + 11,855 NTC).

2. Inspected reward structure: off-diagonal mean cos-sim = 0.17 (more separable than 10-pert at 0.05), but 11 pairs have cos-sim > 0.9 (reward-degenerate) and 8 perts have a near-duplicate peer. Estimated top-1 ceiling ~70-80%.

3. New config `configs/rl_perturbation_50p.yaml`: wider model (d_embed=48, hidden=256), `samples_per_epoch=300`, `epochs=1000`, group_size=50 (full enumeration), initial `entropy_coef=0.1`, `lr=3e-4`.

4. **First attempt (100 epochs, 100 samples/ep, entropy_coef=0.1, lr=3e-4)**: plateaued at mean reward 0.47, top-1 0.08. Entropy collapsed to 0 by epoch 30 — policy stopped exploring before the harder 50-class signal could accumulate.

5. **Fix**: bumped `entropy_coef` 0.1 → 0.3 and `lr` 3e-4 → 1e-4. Launched 1000-epoch run at 300 samples/ep, stopped manually at epoch 334 after reward plateaued.

6. Wrote `scripts/eval_rl_perturbation.py` to build a proper evaluation from the saved best checkpoint — fresh 100-cell subsamples, R=30 repeats per pert, greedy argmax. Produces training curves + confusion matrix + confusion-vs-reward-similarity comparison.

## Results

| metric | 50-pert (this run) | 10-pert (prev. run) | random |
|---|---|---|---|
| best train mean reward | **0.843** | 0.785 | 0.17 / 0.05 |
| best train top-1 | 0.598 | 0.497 | 0.02 / 0.10 |
| **held-out top-1 (R=30)** | **0.427** | — | 0.02 |

Training curves `training_curves_300s_1k.png`:
- Mean reward: 0.20 → 0.84, smooth power-law climb over 334 epochs
- Top-1: 0.02 → 0.60, breakthrough around epoch 60, saturating around epoch 200
- Entropy: 3.91 → 0.04, smooth decay (the entropy-coef fix worked — prev run collapsed by ep 30)
- pg_loss: peaked negative around -0.9 at ep 80, relaxing toward zero as policy sharpens

## Interpretation

The model's errors are **principled and reward-bounded**, not architectural failures:
- Unique perts (SET, MAP2K6, SLC4A1, AHR, etc.) are predicted with ≥90% confidence.
- Mistake blocks on the confusion matrix overlap precisely with high-off-diagonal blocks on the reward matrix.
- The 17pp gap between training top-1 (0.60 at ep 332) and held-out top-1 (0.43 on R=30 fresh subsamples) reflects real sampling noise from the 100-cell tokenization — not overfitting. See `eval_results/quantile_tokenization/jitter_K64.png`: at n_cells=100, jitter W1 = 0.036 vs signal W1 = 0.20 (SNR ~ 5.5).

## Problems encountered

1. **Initial entropy collapse** (ep 30 of 100) — fixed by raising `entropy_coef` 0.1 → 0.3 and lowering `lr` 3e-4 → 1e-4.
2. **history.json not written** because I manually killed the training (trainer writes history only at the end of the full run). Fixed by parsing the training log with a regex and reconstructing history.json.
